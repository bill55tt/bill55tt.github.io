Basic Electricity Chapter 1: Introduction to Electricity

1-1 Characteristics of Electricity

Definition of Electricity

Electricity is a form of energy that is colorless, odorless, soundless, and formless.

During its work process, electricity does not require oxygen, making it pollution-free and environmentally friendly.

All electrical appliances in daily life rely on electrical energy to operate, such as flashlights emitting light, electric pots heating, and computers for internet access.

Formation of Electricity

Natural Formation:

  • Lightning: Accumulation of charges in the atmosphere produces discharge phenomena, as demonstrated by Franklin’s kite experiment, which led to the invention of the lightning rod.
  • Frictional Electricity: Rubbing fur against a plastic rod can generate charges that attract small pieces of paper.
  • Electrostatic Induction: Charged objects can induce charges in neutral objects, such as changing water flow direction with a rubbed plastic rod.

Artificial Formation (Power Generation Methods):

  • Hydropower: Using water flow to drive generators, a clean energy source.
  • Thermal Power: Burning coal, petroleum, and natural gas, high energy but with serious pollution.
  • Wind Power: Using wind energy to drive windmills, clean but weather-dependent.
  • Nuclear Power: Using nuclear fission, high efficiency but with radioactive pollution risks.
  • Solar Power: Converting light energy to electricity through photovoltaic effect, environmentally friendly but low efficiency.

Matter, Molecules, and Atoms

  • Matter: Something that has mass and occupies space.
  • Molecules: The smallest particles that maintain the basic properties of matter (e.g., water molecule H₂O).
  • Atoms: The basic units constituting molecules, such as H (hydrogen atom) and O (oxygen atom).

Summary

Electricity is a form of energy that can improve human life.

Understanding electricity sources (natural vs. artificial) and different power generation methods, each with its advantages and disadvantages.

Matter is composed of molecules, which are further composed of atoms, laying the foundation for learning atomic structure.

基本電學 第一章：電學概論

1-1 電的特性

電的定義

電是一種無色、無味、無聲、無形的能量。

電在做功的過程中不需要氧氣，因此無污染，符合環保需求。

日常生活中所有電器都依賴電能運行，例如：手電筒發光、電鍋加熱、電腦上網等。

電的形成

自然形成：

  • 閃電：大氣中電荷積累產生放電現象，例如富蘭克林的風箏實驗發現電可以被導引，並發明避雷針。
  • 摩擦生電：毛皮摩擦塑膠棒可產生電荷，吸引碎紙片等。
  • 靜電感應：帶電物體可使中性物體產生感應電荷，如摩擦塑膠棒可改變水流方向。

人為形成（發電方式）：

  • 水力發電：利用水流推動發電機，清潔能源。
  • 火力發電：燃燒煤、石油、天然氣，能量大但污染嚴重。
  • 風力發電：利用風能推動風車發電，清潔但受天氣影響。
  • 核能發電：利用核裂變發電，效率高但存在放射性污染風險。
  • 太陽能發電：利用光電效應轉化為電能，環保但效率低。

物質、分子與原子

  • 物質：具有質量和佔據空間的東西。
  • 分子：保持物質基本特性的最小微粒（例如水的分子 H₂O）。
  • 原子：構成分子的基本單位，例如 H（氫原子）、O（氧原子）。

總結

電是一種能量，可以改善人類生活。

了解電的來源（自然 vs. 人為），不同的發電方式各有優缺點。

物質由分子構成，分子進一步由原子組成，為後續學習原子結構打下基礎。
