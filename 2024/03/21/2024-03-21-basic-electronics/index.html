Basic Electronics: Understanding Circuits and Components

Fundamentals of Electricity

  • Electrons are the smallest fundamental particles that constitute electricity, existing within atoms.
  • When electrons leave atoms and flow freely, they form an electric current.
  • Copper, silver, and gold are excellent conductors because they have only one electron in their outermost shell. Copper is widely used in wires due to its availability.
  • Voltage is the pressure that drives electron flow, moving from high voltage to low voltage, measured in Volts (V). Physically, it represents the energy in Joules required to move one Coulomb of charge.
  • Current is the number of charges passing per second, measured in Amperes (A). One ampere equals 6.24150934 × 10¹⁸ electrons per second.
  • Current direction is conventionally defined as the direction of positive charge flow.
  • Power (P) is energy consumed per second, calculated by P = IV (Power = Current × Voltage), measured in Watts (W).

Electric Circuits

  • A circuit is a closed loop of conductors providing a path for current to flow.
  • Kirchhoff’s laws state that the sum of currents entering a junction equals the sum leaving it, and the sum of voltage changes around any closed loop must be zero.
  • Direct Current (DC) has constant voltage magnitude and direction, like batteries.
  • Alternating Current (AC) has voltage magnitude and direction that change periodically with time, like power outlets.
  • A short circuit occurs when there’s no resistance, causing excessive current that can burn out the circuit or cause fires.

Passive Components

  • Resistance opposes current flow, measured in Ohms (Ω).
  • Ohm’s Law describes the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance: V = IR (Voltage = Current × Resistance). Higher resistance means lower current at the same voltage.
  • Resistors provide resistance, but all electronic components, including wires and power sources, have some internal resistance.
  • Capacitors store positive and negative charges under voltage, buffering voltage changes, measured in Farads (F), with formula C = Q/V (Capacitance = Charge / Voltage). They act like reservoirs for DC and conduct for AC.
  • Inductors, mainly coils, use electromagnetic induction to store and release magnetic energy, creating temporary voltage to oppose current changes, smoothing current variations. They act like water pipes with turbines for DC and form impedance for AC. They can also be used for AC voltage transformation.
  • Resistors, capacitors, and inductors are typically passive components that support other components.

Active Components

  • Active components can actively control current flow and switching, typically serving as core logic elements in electronic devices.
  • Semiconductors have properties between conductors and insulators, ideal for precise current control, primarily made of silicon (Si).
  • N-type semiconductors are doped with elements having one extra electron (like phosphorus), making them more likely to lose electrons.
  • P-type semiconductors are doped with elements having one fewer electron (like boron), making them more likely to accept electrons.
  • Diodes allow current flow in only one direction, using semiconductor PN junctions. They conduct under forward voltage and block under reverse voltage, like one-way valves.
  • Transistors use NPN or PNP arrangements, utilizing NP semiconductor concentration differences to achieve current amplification. A small base (B) current controls large current between emitter (E) and collector (C), useful for amplifying analog signals or as digital switches.
  • MOSFETs (Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor Field-Effect Transistors) control signals with voltage. Enhancement-type MOSFETs conduct between source (S) and drain (D) when voltage is applied to the gate (G); depletion-type MOSFETs are normally conductive and close the channel when voltage is applied to the gate.
  • Various logic gates can be built from active components, enabling complex computations through gate combinations.

Electronic Circuit Composition and Applications

  • Complete electronic circuits typically include power sources (heart), passive components (voltage and current stabilization), active components (brain), and input/output components.
  • Input components include switches, buttons, keyboards, variable resistors, photoresistors, thermistors, microphones, etc., for control or sensing external environments.
  • Output components include light bulbs, LEDs, speakers, motors, etc., for visual, audio, or power output.

Power Generation and Distribution

  • Mainstream power generation uses electromagnetic induction, generating current by rotating magnets with kinetic energy.
  • Solar power generation uses diode concepts, creating current when sunlight hits NP semiconductors.
  • Power is typically transmitted at high voltage to reduce losses, then stepped down through multiple stages before entering household distribution boxes.
  • Taiwanese household distribution boxes typically have +110V and -110V hot lines, 0V neutral line, and ground wire for leakage prevention.
  • 220V outlets for air conditioners use both +110V and -110V hot lines.
  • Many appliances need AC to DC conversion, commonly achieved using diode rectification and capacitor smoothing.
  • Common appliances like LED bulbs have built-in AC to DC transformers; fans control current through different inductor stages to drive motors; rice cookers and water heaters convert electrical energy to heat using heating elements.

Summary and Reflection

  • Humans manipulate electricity through simple or complex electronic circuits to produce various products, improving quality of life.
  • While enjoying the benefits of electricity, we should also be aware of its environmental impact and consider sustainable development.

十一分鐘略懂電子電路：電、電路、電阻、電容、電感、二極體、三極體、MOSFET 簡介及相關應用

電的基礎

  • 電子是構成電的最小基本粒子，在原子中存在。
  • 當電子離開原子自由流動時，形成電流。
  • 銅、銀、金因最外層只有一顆電子，容易成為良好導體，其中銅因容易取得而被廣泛使用於導線。
  • 電壓是推動電子流動的壓力，會從高電壓流向低電壓形成電流，單位是伏特 (V)，物理意義是移動每庫倫電荷所需的焦耳能量。
  • 電流是每秒通過的電荷數量，單位是安培 (A)，1 安培等於每秒通過 6.24150934 × 10¹⁸ 個電子。
  • 電流的方向通常以正電荷流的想像方向為準。
  • 功率 (P) 是每秒消耗的能量，可以透過公式 P = IV（功率 = 電流 × 電壓）計算，單位是瓦特 (W)。

電路

  • 電路是由導體組成的封閉迴路，提供電流往返的路徑。
  • 克希荷夫定律說明進入分岔點的電流總和等於離開分岔點的電流總和，且迴路的電壓升降總和必定為 0。
  • 直流電 (DC) 的電壓大小與方向固定，例如電池。
  • 交流電 (AC) 的電壓大小與方向隨時間週期性變化，例如電源插座。
  • 短路是指電路上毫無阻力導致電流過大，可能燒毀電路甚至引起火災。

被動元件

  • 電阻是阻礙電流通過的能力，單位是歐姆 (Ω)。
  • 歐姆定律描述電壓、電流和電阻之間的關係：V = IR（電壓 = 電流 × 電阻）。在相同電壓下，電阻越大電流越小。
  • 電阻器是提供電阻的元件，但任何電子元件甚至導線和電源本身也都有微小電阻。
  • 電容能在電壓帶動下儲存正負電荷，進而緩衝電壓變化，單位是法拉 (F)，公式為 C = Q/V（電容 = 電荷量 / 電壓）。對直流電而言，電容像水庫；對交流電而言，電容則像通路一樣充放電。
  • 電感主要由線圈組成，利用電磁感應儲存與釋放磁能，產生臨時電壓以抵抗電流的改變，有平滑電流變化的效果。對直流電而言，像裝有渦輪的水管；對交流電而言，則形成阻抗。電感也能用於交流電的變壓。
  • 電阻、電容、電感通常作為被動配合輔助其他元件的角色，因此稱為被動元件。

主動元件

  • 主動元件能主動控制電流的流向與開關，通常作為電器中的核心邏輯運算角色。
  • 半導體的特性介於導體和絕緣體之間，適合用來精準控制電流的通過與否，主要材料是矽 (Si)。
  • N 型半導體是摻入多一顆電子的元素（如磷），使其更容易丟失電子。
  • P 型半導體是摻入少一顆電子的元素（如硼），使其更容易吸收電子。
  • 二極體只允許電流單方向通過，利用半導體的 PN 接合實現。順向電壓導通，逆向電壓不導通，像單向水閥。
  • 三極體 (電晶體) 透過 NPN 或 PNP 的排列，利用 NP 型半導體濃度的差異來產生電流放大的效果。基極 (B) 的小電流可以控制射極 (E) 和集極 (C) 之間的大電流，可用於放大類比訊號或作為數位開關。
  • MOSFET (金屬氧化物半導體場效電晶體) 以電壓來控制訊號。增強型 MOS 在閘極 (G) 施加電壓時導通源極 (S) 和汲極 (D) 之間的通道；空乏型 MOS 原本導通，在閘極施加電壓時關閉通道。
  • 基於主動元件可以組合出各種邏輯閘，再透過邏輯閘的搭配進行複雜的運算。

電子電路的組成與應用

  • 完整的電子電路通常包含電源（心臟）、被動元件（穩定電壓與電流）、主動元件（大腦）、以及輸入與輸出元件。
  • 輸入元件例如開關、按鍵、鍵盤、可變電阻、光敏電阻、熱敏電阻、麥克風等，用於控制或感測外部環境。
  • 輸出元件例如燈泡、LED、喇叭、馬達等，用於輸出視覺、聲音或動力。

電力的生產與配送

  • 主流發電原理是電磁感應，利用動能帶動磁鐵轉動產生電流。
  • 太陽能發電利用二極體的概念，讓太陽光照射 NP 半導體產生電流。
  • 電力通常以高壓電輸送以減少損耗，再經過層層降壓後進入家戶配電箱。
  • 台灣家戶配電箱通常有 +110V、-110V 兩條火線和 0V 中性線，以及地線用於預防漏電。
  • 冷氣所需的 220V 插座會同時使用 +110V 和 -110V 的火線。
  • 許多電器需要交流電轉直流電，常見的做法是使用二極體整流，再利用電容緩衝平滑波形。
  • 常見電器如 LED 燈泡內建 AC 轉 DC 變壓器；電扇透過不同段位的電感控制電流驅動馬達；電鍋和熱水器利用加熱片將電能轉為熱能。

總結與反思

  • 人類透過簡單或複雜的電子電路來操縱電力，生產出各式各樣的產品，提升生活品質。
  • 在享受電力帶來好處的同時，也應意識到電力生產對環境的影響，並思考如何兼顧環境的永續經營。
