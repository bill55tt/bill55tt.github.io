Understanding Prefixes for Powers of Ten

Definition

Power of Ten: Represented as \(10^n\), used to simplify numerical representation.

定義

十的冪次方：表示為 \(10^n\)，用於簡化數字表示。

Prefixes and Their Meanings

Prefix Symbol Power Example
Tera T \(10^{12}\) 1 TΩ = \(1 \times 10^{12}\) Ω
Giga G \(10^{9}\) 1 GΩ = \(1 \times 10^{9}\) Ω
Mega M \(10^{6}\) 1 MΩ = \(1 \times 10^{6}\) Ω
Kilo k \(10^{3}\) 1 kΩ = \(1 \times 10^{3}\) Ω
Milli m \(10^{-3}\) 1 mA = \(1 \times 10^{-3}\) A
Micro μ \(10^{-6}\) 1 μF = \(1 \times 10^{-6}\) F
Nano n \(10^{-9}\) 1 nF = \(1 \times 10^{-9}\) F
Pico p \(10^{-12}\) 1 pF = \(1 \times 10^{-12}\) F

字首及其含義

字首 符號 冪次方 例子
兆 T \(10^{12}\) 1 TΩ = \(1 \times 10^{12}\) Ω
十億 G \(10^{9}\) 1 GΩ = \(1 \times 10^{9}\) Ω
百萬 M \(10^{6}\) 1 MΩ = \(1 \times 10^{6}\) Ω
千 k \(10^{3}\) 1 kΩ = \(1 \times 10^{3}\) Ω
毫 m \(10^{-3}\) 1 mA = \(1 \times 10^{-3}\) A
微 μ \(10^{-6}\) 1 μF = \(1 \times 10^{-6}\) F
奈 n \(10^{-9}\) 1 nF = \(1 \times 10^{-9}\) F
皮 p \(10^{-12}\) 1 pF = \(1 \times 10^{-12}\) F

*注意: 台灣對 p ($10^{-12}$) 的翻譯通常為「皮」，而非「漠」

Application

  • Simplifies numerical writing, avoiding errors.
  • Commonly used by engineers and scientists for calculations and communication.

應用

  • 簡化數字書寫，避免錯誤。
  • 工程師和科學家常用於計算和溝通。

Computer Terminology

In computing, the definitions of prefixes differ (based on powers of 2):

  • K (Kilo) = \(2^{10}\) = 1024
  • M (Mega) = \(2^{20}\) = 1,048,576
  • G (Giga) = \(2^{30}\) = 1,073,741,824

電腦術語

在電腦中，字首的定義不同 (基於2的冪次方)：

  • K（千） = \(2^{10}\) = 1024
  • M（百萬） = \(2^{20}\) = 1,048,576
  • G（十億） = \(2^{30}\) = 1,073,741,824

Examples

  • Resistor: 47 kΩ = \(47 \times 10^{3}\) Ω
  • Current: 0.005 A = 5 mA = \(5 \times 10^{-3}\) A

例子

  • 電阻：47 kΩ = \(47 \times 10^{3}\) Ω
  • 電流：0.005 A = 5 mA = \(5 \times 10^{-3}\) A

Atomic Theory Summary

Atomic Theory Summary

1. Structure of Atoms

English:

  • Nucleus: Contains protons and neutrons.
  • Protons carry a positive charge, while neutrons are neutral.
  • Electrons: Carry a negative charge and orbit around the nucleus.

中文:

  • 原子核: 包含質子和中子。
  • 質子帶有正電荷，中子不帶電。
  • 電子: 帶有負電荷，圍繞在原子核外的軌道上。

2. Atomic Number

English:

  • Definition: The atomic number is the number of protons in an atom, which equals the number of electrons.
  • Example: Copper has an atomic number of 29, indicating 29 protons and 29 electrons.

中文:

  • 定義: 原子序是指原子中質子的數量，等於電子的數量。
  • 例子: 銅的原子序為29，表示有29個質子和29個電子。

3. Atomic Orbitals

English:

  • Maximum electron capacity formula: (2N^2), where N is the orbital layer number.
  • First layer (K layer): Can hold up to 2 electrons.
  • Second layer (L layer): Can hold up to 8 electrons.
  • Third layer (M layer): Can hold up to 18 electrons.
  • Fourth layer: Can hold up to 32 electrons.

中文:

  • 最大電子數公式: (2N^2)，其中N為軌道層數。
  • 第一層 (K層): 最多可容納2個電子。
  • 第二層 (L層): 最多可容納8個電子。
  • 第三層 (M層): 最多可容納18個電子。
  • 第四層: 最多可容納32個電子。

4. Valence Electrons

English:

  • Definition: Valence electrons are the outermost electrons that determine the chemical properties and reactivity of an element.
  • Example: Silicon has an atomic number of 14 and 4 valence electrons, meaning it typically forms four covalent bonds in chemical reactions.

中文:

  • 定義: 價電子是最外層的電子，決定元素的化學性質和反應性。
  • 例子: 矽的原子序為14，價電子數為4，這意味著它在化學反應中通常會形成四個共價鍵。

5. Charge and Mass of Particles

English:

Particle Charge (C) Mass (kg)
Electron -1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ 9.107 × 10⁻³¹
Proton +1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ 1.6729 × 10⁻²⁷
Neutron 0 1.675 × 10⁻²⁷

中文:

粒子 電荷 (C) 質量 (kg)
電子 -1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ 9.107 × 10⁻³¹
質子 +1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ 1.6729 × 10⁻²⁷
中子 0 1.675 × 10⁻²⁷

Summary

English:

  • Atoms are electrically neutral, as the number of protons equals the number of electrons.
  • The atomic number is a fundamental characteristic of an element that influences its chemical properties and reactivity.
  • Understanding the structure and properties of atoms is crucial for studying electricity and other scientific fields.

中文:

  • 原子是電中性的，因為質子和電子的數量相等。
  • 原子序是元素的基本特徵，影響其化學性質和反應行為。
  • 理解原子的結構和性質對於學習電學及其他科學領域至關重要。

Summary of Energy and Work

1. Definitions of Energy and Work

English:

  • Energy: The capacity of matter to do work.
  • Work: The process of energy transformation, measured in joules (J).

中文:

  • 能量: 物质做功的能力。
  • 功: 能量的转化过程，单位为焦耳 (J)。

2. Work Calculation

English:

  • Work formula:
    [
    W = F \times S
    ]
    • W: Work (Joules)
    • F: Applied force (Newtons)
    • S: Displacement (meters)

中文:

  • 功的公式：
    [
    W = F \times S
    ]
    • W：功 (焦耳)
    • F：施加的力 (牛顿)
    • S：位移 (米)

3. Example Illustrations

English:

  • Pushing a Handcart:

    • Applying 20 Newtons of force to move 4 meters.
    • Work = 20 N × 4 m = 80 J.

  • Diving Athlete:

    • Weight of 60 kg, potential energy calculation:
      [
      F = m \times g = 60 , \text{kg} \times 9.8 , \text{m/s}^2 = 588 , \text{N}
      ]
    • Work done climbing 5 meters:
      [
      W = F \times h = 588 , \text{N} \times 5 , \text{m} = 2940 , \text{J}
      ]
    • During the dive, potential energy converts to kinetic energy.

中文:

  • 推手推车：

    • 施加20牛顿的力，推动4米。
    • 功 = 20 N × 4 m = 80 J。

  • 跳水选手：

    • 重60公斤，位能计算：
      [
      F = m \times g = 60 , \text{kg} \times 9.8 , \text{m/s}^2 = 588 , \text{N}
      ]
    • 爬升5米的功：
      [
      W = F \times h = 588 , \text{N} \times 5 , \text{m} = 2940 , \text{J}
      ]
    • 跳水时，位能转化为动能。

4. Energy Conversion

English:

  • Electrical energy powers technological products, such as electric cars and televisions, improving human life.

中文:

  • 电能可以驱动科技产品，例如电动汽车和电视，改善人类生活。

PCB Manufacturing Process Guide - Detailed Steps

Posted on 2024-03-23 Edited on 2025-03-24 In Electronics Views: Disqus:

PCB Manufacturing Process Guide

Key Points (English)

  1. Initial Setup

    • Power on the engraving machine (located at the back)
    • Open PCB Prototype software
    • Check file format (AD or PRO)
    • Create new file
    • Select AD engraving area
    • Choose project item from engineering

  2. File Management

    • Two main formats: AD and K
    • AD files for circuit diagrams
    • K files for carving paths
    • File selection by number (e.g., 120220)
    • Path selection (second option)
    • File format: xxx.Protel -GTL

  3. Operation Process

    • Select forming data (4th option from left in top toolbar)
    • Use Page Up (+) to zoom in
    • Use Page End (-) to zoom out
    • Right-click to cancel forming data function
    • Right-click to frame select
    • Left-click to move view
    • Select outer frame (blue line) and inner circles (holes)
    • All selected items turn white

  4. Processing Steps

    • Select forming (5th option from left in top toolbar)
    • Outer frame and inner holes turn into blue circles
    • Outer frame turns green when outermost layer selected
    • Inner holes turn green when innermost layer selected
    • Right-click after green selection
    • Click “Calculate Engraving Logic” (6th option from left)
    • Click “Flip” (7th option from left)
    • Note: Large copper board for single-sided printing
    • Note: Small copper board for double-sided printing

  5. Alignment Process

    • Click “H” (1st option in 2nd row from right)
    • Adjust board position within copper board
    • Right-click for rotation
    • Frame selection turns blue
    • Right-click to rotate
    • Must align within white lines on sides and orange frame at bottom
    • Click “H” again
    • Board alignment: 12.15 on both sides
    • Must have sticker attached

  6. Processing Area Setup

    • Select auto correction engraving area
    • Select processing area (3rd to 6th options in 2nd row from right)
    • Note: Report if area exceeds limits
    • Select auto tool change mode (2nd to last option in 2nd row from right)
    • Set positioning hole drilling
    • Forming tool: 1.4mm
    • Wait for 1 hour

PCB Board Flipping Process

Key Points (English)

  1. Initial Setup

    • Select the 8th option from left in function bar for board flipping
    • Board needs to be flipped accordingly
    • Reapply adhesive tape

  2. CCD Alignment Process

    • Click CCD (last option in first row from right in side toolbar)
    • Use auto-alignment until CCD shows “PASS”
    • Click auto tool change processing mode
    • Select surface detection and carving path
    • Wait for 30 minutes

  3. Drilling Process

    • Use auto tool change mode (second to last option in second row from right)
    • Select “PCB Internal Drilling”
    • Size settings:
      • T1: Depth 2.8mm (standard size)
      • Second drill: 0.4mm (manual change)
      • Place in NO1 = T1
      • Occasionally switches to T2/T3
    • Note: Use simulation tool for holes over 1.5mm
    • Wait for 1 hour

  4. Final Processing

    • Use auto tool change processing mode
    • Select outer frame forming
    • Only change drill bits
    • Auto tool change setting OK
    • Recommended forming tool: 1.4mm

Important Notes

Key Points (English)

  1. Tool Selection

    • Standard depth: 2.8mm
    • Secondary drill: 0.4mm
    • Forming tool: 1.4mm recommended
    • Simulation tool for holes > 1.5mm

  2. Processing Times

    • Surface detection: ~30 minutes
    • Drilling process: ~1 hour
    • Total process time varies by board size

  3. Quality Control

    • Ensure CCD shows “PASS”
    • Check surface detection results
    • Verify drilling depths
    • Monitor outer frame formation

Electronic Components Identification and SMD Soldering Guide

Posted on 2024-03-23 Edited on 2025-03-24 In Electronics Views: Disqus:

Electronic Components Identification

Resistors

  • Flat components ranging from 603 to 25 sizes
  • Have numbered markings on the surface
  • Used for current limiting and voltage division

Capacitors

  • Two-container appearance for power storage
  • Available in sizes like 603, 0806, 10
  • Gray appearance with no polarity
  • Used for filtering and energy storage

Inductors

  • Large brown square blocks
  • Easy to identify
  • No polarity
  • Used for filtering and energy storage

Diodes

  • Triangular marking on the component
  • Polarity sensitive
  • Used for rectification and protection

Transistors

  • Shiny pins
  • Common types include M-type transistors
  • Three pins marked as SPS
  • First pin connects to voltage and ground
  • Available in both small and large module sizes
  • M-type transistors have paired pins at the bottom

Inductors (Large Type)

  • Very large components
  • Easy to identify by size
  • Smaller inductors available upon request

PCB Software Operation Guide

File Types and Setup

  • Two main file formats: AD and K
  • AD files a、e primarily used for circuit diagrams
  • For AD files, create new white data
  • Navigate through component interface options
  • K files are typically in “New Carving Path”
  • AD files are in “AD Carving Path” or “AD Carving Method”

Operation Steps

  1. File Selection

    • Find file by number (e.g., 119296921)
    • Always select the second option
    • Files are provided and placed in the system

  2. Path Selection

    • Select second option for all paths
    • One for J, one for viewing
    • Terminal also uses second option
    • New items also use second option

  3. Data Selection

    • Click “Select Data” and “Select Forming Data”
    • Mouse cursor changes to four arrows
    • Click HG up to zoom
    • Select blue line around PCB
    • Select inner blue line for holes

  4. Processing

    • Click outside first (no holes)
    • Select fifth option for data offset
    • Outer frame becomes white
    • Click fifth option for fixed posts
    • Becomes two circles
    • Outer frame stores outermost
    • Changes to green
    • Right-click after green
    • Click 1-2-3-4-5, sixth is “Calculate Path”
    • Click after calculation

Panel Types

  • Single-sided panel: larger size
  • Double-sided panel: smaller size
  • Can be distinguished by appearance
  • Single panel has one side
  • Double panel has small features on top

Positioning and Calibration

  1. Use “Add Area” for positioning

    • Click four corners
    • Check upper and lower positions
    • Adjust if board is too large
    • Confirm processing mode

  2. Positioning Process

    • Wait for CDC
    • Auto-move to hole alignment
    • P indicates success
    • NG requires manual adjustment
    • Press C for next step
    • Auto tool change processing mode

  3. Surface Detection

    • Perform surface detection
    • Check carving depth
    • Proceed to line carving
    • Check for obvious lines indicating problems
    • Process takes about 10:30 to complete
    • Larger boards take longer

SMD Soldering Guide (1206 Resistor Example)

Temperature Settings

  • Exhaust fan used above 350-360°C
  • Temperature varies by component
  • Higher temperature (400°C+) for faster soldering
  • Lower temperature for slower, more controlled soldering

Basic Steps

  1. Start with 1206 size (about R6R7 edge size)
  2. Solder melts at high temperature
  3. Use dog bone-shaped soldering tip
  4. Always tin the pad first
  5. Use tweezers to hold component
  6. Align and solder one side first
  7. Solder the other side
  8. Initial solder amount may be more
  9. Ensure component is flat on pad
  10. Remove excess solder if needed

Advanced Techniques

  • Place iron close to pad
  • Hold position when heating
  • Use tweezers to hold component
  • Rotate 90 degrees if needed
  • Add flux if soldering is difficult
  • Flip board if needed
  • Heat and add solder simultaneously
  • Clean iron tip with wet sponge
  • Use small paper cup with water
  • Scrape old solder before adding new

Common Issues and Solutions

  • Excess solder: scrape or add new solder
  • Dull iron tip: replace tip
  • Connected solder: heat and press down
  • Dropped components: use tweezers on both sides
  • Keep sponge moist
  • Clean pad before soldering
  • Check for green frame on PCB
  • Ground connection for 0603 resistors without green frame

Safety and Maintenance

  • Place iron on stand when not in use
  • Don’t place directly on table
  • Clean excess solder gradually
  • Add new solder if needed
  • Replace tip when it loses shine
  • Keep sponge wet
  • Take breaks as needed

電子元件辨識指南

電阻

  • 從 603 到最大的 25 尺寸都很平
  • 零件上面會有編號
  • 用於限流和分壓

電容

  • 外觀像是兩個容器，用於儲存電力
  • 規格有 0603、0806、1206、1210 等
  • 外觀是兩個灰色的樣子
  • 沒有正負極之分
  • 用於濾波和能量儲存

電感

  • 看起來就是一個很大顆的茶色方塊
  • 容易辨識
  • 沒有正負極之分
  • 用於濾波和能量儲存

二極體

  • 在零件上會有三角形的標識
  • 有極性之分
  • 用於整流和保護

電晶體

  • 腳位通常是閃亮的
  • 最常用的是 M 型電晶體
  • 有三個腳位，標示為 SPS
  • 第一個腳位連接電壓和接地
  • 有大尺寸和小尺寸模組
  • M 型電晶體的配對腳位在底部

電感（大尺寸）

  • 超大尺寸元件
  • 容易從大小辨識

Basic Electricity Chapter 1:PARTA Atomic Structure and Properties

Atomic Structure

  • The center of an atom contains a nucleus, which consists of positively charged protons and electrically neutral neutrons.
  • Negatively charged electrons orbit around the nucleus. For teaching purposes, different orbits are distinguished by different colors.
  • An atom is electrically neutral because the positive charge of protons in the nucleus equals and cancels out the negative charge of electrons outside the nucleus.
  • Electrons in the outermost orbit are called valence electrons, and their number determines the element’s valence in the periodic table.

Atomic Number

  • The atomic number represents the number of protons in the atomic nucleus.
  • In a neutral atom, the atomic number also equals the number of electrons outside the nucleus.
  • The periodic table is arranged according to atomic numbers.
  • Examples: Hydrogen (H) has atomic number 1, meaning it has 1 proton and 1 electron. An atom with atomic number 5 has 5 protons and 5 electrons.
  • Common element examples: Copper (Cu) has atomic number 29 (29 protons and 29 electrons), Germanium (Ge) has atomic number 32 (32 protons and 32 electrons), and Silicon (Si) has atomic number 14 (14 protons and 14 electrons).

Electron Orbital Distribution

  • Electrons outside the nucleus are distributed in different orbits in layers.
  • The maximum number of electrons each layer can hold can be calculated using the formula 2n², where n is the orbit number (n=1, 2, 3, 4…).
  • This formula is only applicable for orbits n ≤ 4.
  • The first layer (n=1), also called K shell, can hold up to 2 × (1)² = 2 electrons.
  • The second layer (n=2), also called L shell, can hold up to 2 × (2)² = 8 electrons.
  • The third layer (n=3), also called M shell, can hold up to 2 × (3)² = 18 electrons.
  • The fourth layer (n=4), also called N shell, can hold up to 2 × (4)² = 32 electrons.
  • The outermost electron layer is called the valence shell.
  • Example: Silicon (Si) has atomic number 14, with electron distribution: 2 electrons in K shell, 8 electrons in L shell, and 4 electrons in M shell. Therefore, silicon has 4 valence electrons and is a tetravalent atom.

Electric Charge and Mass

  • The unit of electric charge is Coulomb (C).
  • Electrons carry negative charge of -1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ Coulombs.
  • Protons carry positive charge of +1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ Coulombs. The charge magnitudes of electrons and protons are equal but opposite in sign.
  • Neutrons carry no charge (0).
  • The unit of mass is kilograms (kg).
  • Electron mass is approximately 9.107 × 10⁻³¹ kg.
  • Proton mass is approximately 1.6729 × 10⁻²⁷ kg.
  • Proton mass is much larger than electron mass, about 1840 times greater.

Summary and Recommendations

  • This lesson introduced the basic structure of atoms.
  • Students are recommended to practice drawing atomic structures of Copper (Cu), Germanium (Ge), and Silicon (Si), showing electron distribution and valence electrons, using the 2n² formula.

基本電學 第一章：PARTA 原子結構與特性

原子結構

  • 原子中心有一個原子核，原子核由帶正電荷的質子和不帶電荷的中子組成。
  • 帶負電荷的電子在原子核外的軌道上運行。為了方便講解，軌道會用不同顏色區分。
  • 原子是電中性的，因為原子核內質子的正電荷與核外電子的負電荷數量相等且互相抵消。
  • 最外層軌道的電子稱為價電子，價電子的數量決定了該元素在元素週期表上的價數。

原子序

  • 原子序代表了該原子核中的質子數量。
  • 在電中性的原子中，原子序也等於核外電子的數量。
  • 元素週期表是根據原子序排列的。
  • 例如：原子序為 1 的氫原子，其原子核中含有 1 個質子，核外也有 1 個電子。原子序為 5 的原子，其原子核中有 5 個質子，核外有 5 個電子。
  • 常見元素的原子序例子：銅 (Cu) 的原子序是 29，表示其有 29 個質子和 29 個電子；鍺 (Ge) 的原子序是 32，表示其有 32 個質子和 32 個電子；矽 (Si) 的原子序是 14，表示其有 14 個質子和 14 個電子。

原子軌道分佈

  • 核外電子是分層分佈在不同的軌道上。
  • 每個電子層能容納的最大電子數可以用公式 2n² 計算，其中 n 是軌道數目（n=1, 2, 3, 4…）。
  • 這個公式只適用於軌道數小於等於 4 的情況。
  • 第一層 (n=1) 又稱為 K 層，最多可容納 2 × (1)² = 2 個電子。
  • 第二層 (n=2) 又稱為 L 層，最多可容納 2 × (2)² = 8 個電子。
  • 第三層 (n=3) 又稱為 M 層，最多可容納 2 × (3)² = 18 個電子。
  • 第四層 (n=4) 又稱為 N 層，最多可容納 2 × (4)² = 32 個電子。
  • 最外層的電子層又稱為價層。
  • 矽 (Si) 的原子序是 14，其電子分佈為：第一層 (K) 2 個電子，第二層 (L) 8 個電子，第三層 (M) 4 個電子。因此，矽原子最外層有 4 個價電子，是四價原子。

帶電量與質量

  • 電荷的單位是庫侖 (Coulomb)。
  • 電子帶負電荷，電荷量為 -1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ 庫侖。
  • 質子帶正電荷，電荷量為 +1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ 庫侖。電子和質子的電荷量大小相等，符號相反。
  • 中子不帶電荷，電荷量為 0。
  • 質量的單位是公斤 (kg)。
  • 電子的質量約為 9.107 × 10⁻³¹ 公斤。
  • 質子的質量約為 1.6729 × 10⁻²⁷ 公斤。
  • 質子的質量遠大於電子的質量，約為電子的 1840 倍。

總結與建議

  • 本次課程介紹了原子的基本結構。
  • 建議同學練習繪製銅 (Cu)、鍺 (Ge) 和矽 (Si) 的原子結構圖，標示出電子分佈和價電子，並善用 2n² 公式。

Basic Electricity Chapter 1: Introduction to Electricity

1-1 Characteristics of Electricity

Definition of Electricity

Electricity is a form of energy that is colorless, odorless, soundless, and formless.

During its work process, electricity does not require oxygen, making it pollution-free and environmentally friendly.

All electrical appliances in daily life rely on electrical energy to operate, such as flashlights emitting light, electric pots heating, and computers for internet access.

Formation of Electricity

Natural Formation:

  • Lightning: Accumulation of charges in the atmosphere produces discharge phenomena, as demonstrated by Franklin’s kite experiment, which led to the invention of the lightning rod.
  • Frictional Electricity: Rubbing fur against a plastic rod can generate charges that attract small pieces of paper.
  • Electrostatic Induction: Charged objects can induce charges in neutral objects, such as changing water flow direction with a rubbed plastic rod.

Artificial Formation (Power Generation Methods):

  • Hydropower: Using water flow to drive generators, a clean energy source.
  • Thermal Power: Burning coal, petroleum, and natural gas, high energy but with serious pollution.
  • Wind Power: Using wind energy to drive windmills, clean but weather-dependent.
  • Nuclear Power: Using nuclear fission, high efficiency but with radioactive pollution risks.
  • Solar Power: Converting light energy to electricity through photovoltaic effect, environmentally friendly but low efficiency.

Matter, Molecules, and Atoms

  • Matter: Something that has mass and occupies space.
  • Molecules: The smallest particles that maintain the basic properties of matter (e.g., water molecule H₂O).
  • Atoms: The basic units constituting molecules, such as H (hydrogen atom) and O (oxygen atom).

Summary

Electricity is a form of energy that can improve human life.

Understanding electricity sources (natural vs. artificial) and different power generation methods, each with its advantages and disadvantages.

Matter is composed of molecules, which are further composed of atoms, laying the foundation for learning atomic structure.

基本電學 第一章：電學概論

1-1 電的特性

電的定義

電是一種無色、無味、無聲、無形的能量。

電在做功的過程中不需要氧氣，因此無污染，符合環保需求。

日常生活中所有電器都依賴電能運行，例如：手電筒發光、電鍋加熱、電腦上網等。

電的形成

自然形成：

  • 閃電：大氣中電荷積累產生放電現象，例如富蘭克林的風箏實驗發現電可以被導引，並發明避雷針。
  • 摩擦生電：毛皮摩擦塑膠棒可產生電荷，吸引碎紙片等。
  • 靜電感應：帶電物體可使中性物體產生感應電荷，如摩擦塑膠棒可改變水流方向。

人為形成（發電方式）：

  • 水力發電：利用水流推動發電機，清潔能源。
  • 火力發電：燃燒煤、石油、天然氣，能量大但污染嚴重。
  • 風力發電：利用風能推動風車發電，清潔但受天氣影響。
  • 核能發電：利用核裂變發電，效率高但存在放射性污染風險。
  • 太陽能發電：利用光電效應轉化為電能，環保但效率低。

物質、分子與原子

  • 物質：具有質量和佔據空間的東西。
  • 分子：保持物質基本特性的最小微粒（例如水的分子 H₂O）。
  • 原子：構成分子的基本單位，例如 H（氫原子）、O（氧原子）。

總結

電是一種能量，可以改善人類生活。

了解電的來源（自然 vs. 人為），不同的發電方式各有優缺點。

物質由分子構成，分子進一步由原子組成，為後續學習原子結構打下基礎。

Basic Electronics: Understanding Circuits and Components

Fundamentals of Electricity

  • Electrons are the smallest fundamental particles that constitute electricity, existing within atoms.
  • When electrons leave atoms and flow freely, they form an electric current.
  • Copper, silver, and gold are excellent conductors because they have only one electron in their outermost shell. Copper is widely used in wires due to its availability.
  • Voltage is the pressure that drives electron flow, moving from high voltage to low voltage, measured in Volts (V). Physically, it represents the energy in Joules required to move one Coulomb of charge.
  • Current is the number of charges passing per second, measured in Amperes (A). One ampere equals 6.24150934 × 10¹⁸ electrons per second.
  • Current direction is conventionally defined as the direction of positive charge flow.
  • Power (P) is energy consumed per second, calculated by P = IV (Power = Current × Voltage), measured in Watts (W).

Electric Circuits

  • A circuit is a closed loop of conductors providing a path for current to flow.
  • Kirchhoff’s laws state that the sum of currents entering a junction equals the sum leaving it, and the sum of voltage changes around any closed loop must be zero.
  • Direct Current (DC) has constant voltage magnitude and direction, like batteries.
  • Alternating Current (AC) has voltage magnitude and direction that change periodically with time, like power outlets.
  • A short circuit occurs when there’s no resistance, causing excessive current that can burn out the circuit or cause fires.

Passive Components

  • Resistance opposes current flow, measured in Ohms (Ω).
  • Ohm’s Law describes the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance: V = IR (Voltage = Current × Resistance). Higher resistance means lower current at the same voltage.
  • Resistors provide resistance, but all electronic components, including wires and power sources, have some internal resistance.
  • Capacitors store positive and negative charges under voltage, buffering voltage changes, measured in Farads (F), with formula C = Q/V (Capacitance = Charge / Voltage). They act like reservoirs for DC and conduct for AC.
  • Inductors, mainly coils, use electromagnetic induction to store and release magnetic energy, creating temporary voltage to oppose current changes, smoothing current variations. They act like water pipes with turbines for DC and form impedance for AC. They can also be used for AC voltage transformation.
  • Resistors, capacitors, and inductors are typically passive components that support other components.

Active Components

  • Active components can actively control current flow and switching, typically serving as core logic elements in electronic devices.
  • Semiconductors have properties between conductors and insulators, ideal for precise current control, primarily made of silicon (Si).
  • N-type semiconductors are doped with elements having one extra electron (like phosphorus), making them more likely to lose electrons.
  • P-type semiconductors are doped with elements having one fewer electron (like boron), making them more likely to accept electrons.
  • Diodes allow current flow in only one direction, using semiconductor PN junctions. They conduct under forward voltage and block under reverse voltage, like one-way valves.
  • Transistors use NPN or PNP arrangements, utilizing NP semiconductor concentration differences to achieve current amplification. A small base (B) current controls large current between emitter (E) and collector (C), useful for amplifying analog signals or as digital switches.
  • MOSFETs (Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor Field-Effect Transistors) control signals with voltage. Enhancement-type MOSFETs conduct between source (S) and drain (D) when voltage is applied to the gate (G); depletion-type MOSFETs are normally conductive and close the channel when voltage is applied to the gate.
  • Various logic gates can be built from active components, enabling complex computations through gate combinations.

Electronic Circuit Composition and Applications

  • Complete electronic circuits typically include power sources (heart), passive components (voltage and current stabilization), active components (brain), and input/output components.
  • Input components include switches, buttons, keyboards, variable resistors, photoresistors, thermistors, microphones, etc., for control or sensing external environments.
  • Output components include light bulbs, LEDs, speakers, motors, etc., for visual, audio, or power output.

Power Generation and Distribution

  • Mainstream power generation uses electromagnetic induction, generating current by rotating magnets with kinetic energy.
  • Solar power generation uses diode concepts, creating current when sunlight hits NP semiconductors.
  • Power is typically transmitted at high voltage to reduce losses, then stepped down through multiple stages before entering household distribution boxes.
  • Taiwanese household distribution boxes typically have +110V and -110V hot lines, 0V neutral line, and ground wire for leakage prevention.
  • 220V outlets for air conditioners use both +110V and -110V hot lines.
  • Many appliances need AC to DC conversion, commonly achieved using diode rectification and capacitor smoothing.
  • Common appliances like LED bulbs have built-in AC to DC transformers; fans control current through different inductor stages to drive motors; rice cookers and water heaters convert electrical energy to heat using heating elements.

Summary and Reflection

  • Humans manipulate electricity through simple or complex electronic circuits to produce various products, improving quality of life.
  • While enjoying the benefits of electricity, we should also be aware of its environmental impact and consider sustainable development.

十一分鐘略懂電子電路：電、電路、電阻、電容、電感、二極體、三極體、MOSFET 簡介及相關應用

電的基礎

  • 電子是構成電的最小基本粒子，在原子中存在。
  • 當電子離開原子自由流動時，形成電流。
  • 銅、銀、金因最外層只有一顆電子，容易成為良好導體，其中銅因容易取得而被廣泛使用於導線。
  • 電壓是推動電子流動的壓力，會從高電壓流向低電壓形成電流，單位是伏特 (V)，物理意義是移動每庫倫電荷所需的焦耳能量。
  • 電流是每秒通過的電荷數量，單位是安培 (A)，1 安培等於每秒通過 6.24150934 × 10¹⁸ 個電子。
  • 電流的方向通常以正電荷流的想像方向為準。
  • 功率 (P) 是每秒消耗的能量，可以透過公式 P = IV（功率 = 電流 × 電壓）計算，單位是瓦特 (W)。

電路

  • 電路是由導體組成的封閉迴路，提供電流往返的路徑。
  • 克希荷夫定律說明進入分岔點的電流總和等於離開分岔點的電流總和，且迴路的電壓升降總和必定為 0。
  • 直流電 (DC) 的電壓大小與方向固定，例如電池。
  • 交流電 (AC) 的電壓大小與方向隨時間週期性變化，例如電源插座。
  • 短路是指電路上毫無阻力導致電流過大，可能燒毀電路甚至引起火災。

被動元件

  • 電阻是阻礙電流通過的能力，單位是歐姆 (Ω)。
  • 歐姆定律描述電壓、電流和電阻之間的關係：V = IR（電壓 = 電流 × 電阻）。在相同電壓下，電阻越大電流越小。
  • 電阻器是提供電阻的元件，但任何電子元件甚至導線和電源本身也都有微小電阻。
  • 電容能在電壓帶動下儲存正負電荷，進而緩衝電壓變化，單位是法拉 (F)，公式為 C = Q/V（電容 = 電荷量 / 電壓）。對直流電而言，電容像水庫；對交流電而言，電容則像通路一樣充放電。
  • 電感主要由線圈組成，利用電磁感應儲存與釋放磁能，產生臨時電壓以抵抗電流的改變，有平滑電流變化的效果。對直流電而言，像裝有渦輪的水管；對交流電而言，則形成阻抗。電感也能用於交流電的變壓。
  • 電阻、電容、電感通常作為被動配合輔助其他元件的角色，因此稱為被動元件。

主動元件

  • 主動元件能主動控制電流的流向與開關，通常作為電器中的核心邏輯運算角色。
  • 半導體的特性介於導體和絕緣體之間，適合用來精準控制電流的通過與否，主要材料是矽 (Si)。
  • N 型半導體是摻入多一顆電子的元素（如磷），使其更容易丟失電子。
  • P 型半導體是摻入少一顆電子的元素（如硼），使其更容易吸收電子。
  • 二極體只允許電流單方向通過，利用半導體的 PN 接合實現。順向電壓導通，逆向電壓不導通，像單向水閥。
  • 三極體 (電晶體) 透過 NPN 或 PNP 的排列，利用 NP 型半導體濃度的差異來產生電流放大的效果。基極 (B) 的小電流可以控制射極 (E) 和集極 (C) 之間的大電流，可用於放大類比訊號或作為數位開關。
  • MOSFET (金屬氧化物半導體場效電晶體) 以電壓來控制訊號。增強型 MOS 在閘極 (G) 施加電壓時導通源極 (S) 和汲極 (D) 之間的通道；空乏型 MOS 原本導通，在閘極施加電壓時關閉通道。
  • 基於主動元件可以組合出各種邏輯閘，再透過邏輯閘的搭配進行複雜的運算。

電子電路的組成與應用

  • 完整的電子電路通常包含電源（心臟）、被動元件（穩定電壓與電流）、主動元件（大腦）、以及輸入與輸出元件。
  • 輸入元件例如開關、按鍵、鍵盤、可變電阻、光敏電阻、熱敏電阻、麥克風等，用於控制或感測外部環境。
  • 輸出元件例如燈泡、LED、喇叭、馬達等，用於輸出視覺、聲音或動力。

電力的生產與配送

  • 主流發電原理是電磁感應，利用動能帶動磁鐵轉動產生電流。
  • 太陽能發電利用二極體的概念，讓太陽光照射 NP 半導體產生電流。
  • 電力通常以高壓電輸送以減少損耗，再經過層層降壓後進入家戶配電箱。
  • 台灣家戶配電箱通常有 +110V、-110V 兩條火線和 0V 中性線，以及地線用於預防漏電。
  • 冷氣所需的 220V 插座會同時使用 +110V 和 -110V 的火線。
  • 許多電器需要交流電轉直流電，常見的做法是使用二極體整流，再利用電容緩衝平滑波形。
  • 常見電器如 LED 燈泡內建 AC 轉 DC 變壓器；電扇透過不同段位的電感控制電流驅動馬達；電鍋和熱水器利用加熱片將電能轉為熱能。

總結與反思

  • 人類透過簡單或複雜的電子電路來操縱電力，生產出各式各樣的產品，提升生活品質。
  • 在享受電力帶來好處的同時，也應意識到電力生產對環境的影響，並思考如何兼顧環境的永續經營。
