High School Basic Electricity: Resistance and Resistivity Example - 高中基本電學：電阻與電阻係數範例

Posted on 2025-05-05 In Electronics , Physics , Basic Electronics Views: Disqus:

Introduction

This article demonstrates a practical example of calculating the resistance of a copper wire, showing the step-by-step process of applying the resistance formula and unit conversions.

引言

本文展示了一個計算銅線電阻的實際範例，說明如何應用電阻公式和進行單位轉換的步驟。

Key Points

  1. Problem Background:
    Example: Calculate the resistance of a copper wire that is 2 kilometers long and has a cross-sectional area of 14 square millimeters.
  2. Parameter Identification:
    Length \( L = 2000 \, \text{m} \) (converted from kilometers)
    Cross-sectional area \( A = 14 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{m}^2 \) (converted from square millimeters)
    Resistivity of copper \( \rho = 1.724 \times 10^{-8} \, \Omega \cdot \text{m} \)
  3. Resistance Formula:
    Using the formula \( R = \frac{\rho L}{A} \)
    Calculated resistance \( R = 2.463 \, \Omega \)
  4. Unit Conversion:
    Ensure all parameters are in MKS units (meters, kilograms, seconds).

重點歸納

  1. 題目背景：
    例題：計算一條長2公里、截面積14平方公釐的銅線電阻。
  2. 參數標示：
    長度 \( L = 2000 \, \text{m} \)（轉換自公里）
    截面積 \( A = 14 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{m}^2 \)（轉換自平方公釐）
    銅的電阻係數 \( \rho = 1.724 \times 10^{-8} \, \Omega \cdot \text{m} \)
  3. 電阻公式：
    使用公式 \( R = \frac{\rho L}{A} \)
    計算得出電阻 \( R = 2.463 \, \Omega \)
  4. 單位轉換：
    確保所有參數使用MKS制（米、公斤、秒）。

High School Basic Electricity: Resistance and Resistivity - 高中基本電學：電阻與電阻係數

Posted on 2025-05-05 In Electronics , Physics , Basic Electronics Views: Disqus:

Introduction

This article explores the fundamental concepts of electrical resistance and resistivity, including their definitions, mathematical relationships, and practical applications in electrical engineering.

引言

本文探討電阻與電阻係數的基本概念，包括其定義、數學關係以及在電氣工程中的實際應用。

Key Points

  1. Definition of Resistance:
    Resistance is the opposition encountered by electric current flowing through a material, measured in Ohms (Ω).
  2. Factors Affecting Resistance:
    Resistivity (ρ): The resistance capability of different materials.
    Length (L): Longer lengths result in higher resistance.
    Cross-sectional Area (A): Larger areas lead to lower resistance.
  3. Mathematical Formula:
    The formula for calculating resistance is:
    $$R = \frac{\rho \cdot L}{A}$$
    Where R is resistance, ρ is resistivity, L is length, and A is cross-sectional area.
  4. Relationship Between Resistance and Conductance:
    Conductance is the reciprocal of resistance, indicating the ability to conduct electricity.
  5. Practical Applications:
    Engineers use resistors to control current in circuits, ensuring stable operation.

重點歸納

  1. 電阻的定義：
    電阻是電流在物體中流動時所遇到的阻力，單位為歐姆（Ω）。
  2. 電阻的影響因素：
    電阻係數（ρ）：不同材質的電阻能力。
    物體長度（L）：長度越長，電阻越大。
    截面積（A）：截面積越大，電阻越小。
  3. 數學公式：
    電阻的計算公式為：
    $$R = \frac{\rho \cdot L}{A}$$
    其中，R為電阻，ρ為電阻係數，L為長度，A為截面積。
  4. 電阻與電導的關係：
    電導是電阻的倒數，表示導電能力。
  5. 實際應用：
    工程師利用電阻來控制電路中的電流，確保穩定操作。

High School Basic Electricity: Fundamental Theories of Electricity - Current Part B - 高中基本電學：電學基礎理論 - 電流 PART B

Posted on 2024-05-05 Edited on 2025-05-05 In Electronics , Physics , Basic Electronics Views: Disqus:

Introduction

Current is a fundamental concept in electricity that describes the flow of electric charge. This article explores an alternative method for calculating current based on the physical properties of conductors and electron movement.

引言

電流是電學中的基本概念，描述電荷的流動。本文探討基於導體物理特性和電子運動的電流計算替代方法。

Current Definition and Basic Formula

Current (I) is defined as the amount of charge (Q) passing through a conductor's cross-section per unit time (t):

I = Q/t

電流定義與基本公式

電流(I)定義為單位時間(t)內通過導體截面的電荷量(Q)：

I = Q/t

Alternative Current Calculation Method

Current can also be calculated using the following formula:

I = N × A × v × e

Where:

  • N: Number of free electrons per cubic meter
  • A: Cross-sectional area of the conductor (m²)
  • v: Velocity of electron flow (m/s)
  • e: Charge of an electron (approximately 1.6 × 10^-19 coulombs)

電流計算的替代方法

電流也可以使用以下公式計算：

I = N × A × v × e

其中：

  • N：每立方公尺的自由電子數量
  • A：導體的截面積（m²）
  • v：電子的移動速率（m/s）
  • e：電子的電荷量（約1.6 × 10^-19庫侖）

Calculation Examples

Example 1: Current Calculation in Copper Wire

Given:

  • Free electron density (N) = 8.5 × 10^28 electrons/m³
  • Cross-sectional area (A) = 1 × 10^-6 m²
  • Electron velocity (v) = 0.1 mm/s

Calculate the current:

I = N × A × v × e

I = (8.5 × 10^28) × (1 × 10^-6) × (0.1 × 10^-3) × (1.6 × 10^-19)

I = 1.36 A

Example 2: Electron Velocity Calculation

Given:

  • Current (I) = 2 A
  • Free electron density (N) = 8.5 × 10^28 electrons/m³
  • Cross-sectional area (A) = 2 × 10^-6 m²

Calculate the electron velocity:

v = I / (N × A × e)

v = 2 / [(8.5 × 10^28) × (2 × 10^-6) × (1.6 × 10^-19)]

v ≈ 0.074 mm/s

計算範例

範例1：銅線中的電流計算

已知：

  • 自由電子密度(N) = 8.5 × 10^28 電子/m³
  • 截面積(A) = 1 × 10^-6 m²
  • 電子速度(v) = 0.1 mm/s

計算電流：

I = N × A × v × e

I = (8.5 × 10^28) × (1 × 10^-6) × (0.1 × 10^-3) × (1.6 × 10^-19)

I = 1.36 A

範例2：電子速度計算

已知：

  • 電流(I) = 2 A
  • 自由電子密度(N) = 8.5 × 10^28 電子/m³
  • 截面積(A) = 2 × 10^-6 m²

計算電子速度：

v = I / (N × A × e)

v = 2 / [(8.5 × 10^28) × (2 × 10^-6) × (1.6 × 10^-19)]

v ≈ 0.074 mm/s

Summary

Understanding current calculation methods is essential for electrical engineering

Both basic and alternative formulas provide valuable insights

Proper unit conversion is crucial for accurate calculations

Physical properties of conductors affect current flow

總結

理解電流計算方法對電機工程至關重要

基本公式和替代公式都提供了寶貴的見解

正確的單位轉換對準確計算至關重要

導體的物理特性影響電流流動

High School Basic Electricity: Fundamental Theories of Electricity - Charge Part A - 高中基本電學：電學基礎理論 - 電荷 PART A

Posted on 2024-03-25 Edited on 2025-05-05 In Electronics , Physics , Basic Electronics Views: Disqus:

Introduction

The study of electricity begins with understanding the fundamental concept of electric charge. This article explores the historical development of charge theory and introduces key scientific contributions that shaped our understanding of electricity.

引言

電學的研究始於理解電荷的基本概念。本文探討電荷理論的歷史發展，並介紹塑造我們對電學理解的關鍵科學貢獻。

Historical Background

The understanding of electric charge has evolved through the contributions of several important scientists:

Sir Isaac Newton (1643-1727): His work on universal gravitation influenced later theories of electrical forces

Charles-Augustin de Coulomb (1736-1806): Formulated Coulomb's law describing electrical forces

John Dalton (1766-1844): Proposed atomic theory, though later modified

Robert Millikan (1868-1953): Measured the charge of a single electron

Ernest Rutherford (1871-1937): Contributed to understanding atomic structure

歷史背景

對電荷的理解通過幾位重要科學家的貢獻而發展：

艾薩克・牛頓爵士(1643-1727)：他對萬有引力的研究影響了後來的電力理論

查爾斯・奧古斯丁・庫侖(1736-1806)：提出描述電力作用的庫侖定律

約翰・道耳吞(1766-1844)：提出原子理論，後被修正

羅伯特・密利根(1868-1953)：測量單一電子的電荷

歐內斯特・拉塞福(1871-1937)：對理解原子結構做出貢獻

Basic Charge and Electric Quantity

The fundamental unit of electric charge is the coulomb (C)

One electron carries a charge of -1.6 × 10^-19 coulombs

One proton carries a charge of +1.6 × 10^-19 coulombs

The charge of an electron is equal in magnitude but opposite in sign to that of a proton

基本電荷與電量

電量的基本單位是庫侖(C)

一個電子帶有-1.6 × 10^-19庫侖的電量

一個質子帶有+1.6 × 10^-19庫侖的電量

電子的電量與質子大小相等但符號相反

Key Scientific Contributions

Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation

Influenced the development of Coulomb's law

Demonstrated the concept of action at a distance

Established mathematical relationships for forces

Coulomb's Law

Describes the force between two point charges

Like charges repel, unlike charges attract

Force is proportional to the product of charges

Force is inversely proportional to the square of distance

Dalton's Atomic Theory

Atoms are indivisible (later revised)

All atoms of an element are identical

Atoms combine in simple whole number ratios

Millikan's Oil Drop Experiment

Measured the charge of a single electron

Determined the basic charge unit

Confirmed the quantization of electric charge

關鍵科學貢獻

牛頓的萬有引力定律

影響了庫侖定律的發展

展示了超距作用的概念

建立了力的數學關係

庫侖定律

描述兩個點電荷之間的力

同性相斥，異性相吸

力與電荷乘積成正比

力與距離平方成反比

道耳吞的原子理論

原子是不可分割的（後被修正）

同一元素的所有原子都相同

原子以簡單整數比結合

密利根的油滴實驗

測量單一電子的電荷

確定了基本電荷單位

證實了電荷的量子化

Charge Calculations

Example 1: Total Charge Calculation

Calculate the total charge of 5 × 10^19 electrons:

Q = 5 × 10^19 × (-1.6 × 10^-19)

Q = -8 coulombs

The negative sign indicates excess electrons

Example 2: Number of Electrons

Calculate the number of electrons in 1 coulomb:

Number of electrons = 1 / (1.6 × 10^-19)

Number of electrons = 6.25 × 10^18

電荷計算

範例1：總電量計算

計算5 × 10^19個電子的總電量：

Q = 5 × 10^19 × (-1.6 × 10^-19)

Q = -8庫侖

負號表示過量的電子

範例2：電子數量

計算1庫侖中的電子數量：

電子數量 = 1 / (1.6 × 10^-19)

電子數量 = 6.25 × 10^18

Summary

Electric charge is a fundamental property of matter

The basic unit of charge is the coulomb

Charges interact through Coulomb's law

Understanding charge is essential for electricity

Historical experiments shaped our understanding

總結

電荷是物質的基本屬性

電量的基本單位是庫侖

電荷通過庫侖定律相互作用

理解電荷對電學至關重要

歷史實驗塑造了我們的理解

High School Basic Electricity: Characteristics of Static Charge and Example Exercises - 高中基本電學：靜電荷的特性與範例練習

Posted on 2024-03-25 Edited on 2025-05-05 In Electronics , Physics , Basic Electronics Views: Disqus:

Introduction

Static charge is a fundamental concept in electricity that helps us understand the behavior of electric charges at rest. This article explores the basic characteristics of static charge, including the concepts of electrons and coulombs, and provides practical examples to enhance understanding.

引言

靜電荷是電學中的基本概念，幫助我們理解靜止狀態下電荷的行為。本文將探討靜電荷的基本特性，包括電子和庫侖的概念，並提供實際範例以加深理解。

Basic Unit of Electric Charge

The fundamental unit of electric charge is the coulomb (C)

One electron carries a charge of -1.6 × 10^-19 coulombs

One proton carries a charge of +1.6 × 10^-19 coulombs

The charge of an electron is equal in magnitude but opposite in sign to that of a proton

電量的基本單位

電量的基本單位是庫侖(C)

一個電子帶有-1.6 × 10^-19庫侖的電量

一個質子帶有+1.6 × 10^-19庫侖的電量

電子的電量與質子大小相等但符號相反

Definition of Static Charge

Static charge refers to electric charge that is at rest

It is the accumulation of electric charge on the surface of an object

Static charge can be generated through friction, contact, or induction

The charge remains stationary until it is discharged or neutralized

靜電荷的定義

靜電荷指的是靜止不動的電荷

它是物體表面電荷的累積

靜電荷可以通過摩擦、接觸或感應產生

電荷保持靜止直到被放電或中和

Characteristics of Static Charge

Like Charges Repel

Charges of the same type repel each other

Positive charges repel other positive charges

Negative charges repel other negative charges

This is a fundamental property of electric charges

Opposite Charges Attract

Charges of different types attract each other

Positive and negative charges are attracted to each other

This attraction is the basis of many electrical phenomena

The force of attraction increases with decreasing distance

Electrostatic Induction

Neutral objects can be affected by nearby charged objects

Charges in the neutral object redistribute themselves

This creates temporary charge separation

The effect is stronger at closer distances

靜電荷的特性

同性相斥

相同類型的電荷互相排斥

正電荷排斥其他正電荷

負電荷排斥其他負電荷

這是電荷的基本特性

異性相吸

不同類型的電荷互相吸引

正電荷和負電荷互相吸引

這種吸引力是許多電學現象的基礎

吸引力隨距離減少而增加

靜電感應

中性物體會受到附近帶電物體的影響

中性物體中的電荷會重新分布

這會產生暫時的電荷分離

距離越近，效果越強

Franklin's Experiment

Benjamin Franklin conducted experiments with static electricity

He rubbed a glass rod with silk to generate static charge

The glass rod became positively charged

The silk became negatively charged

This experiment demonstrated the transfer of electrons

富蘭克林的實驗

班傑明・富蘭克林進行了靜電實驗

他通過摩擦玻璃棒和絲綢產生靜電

玻璃棒帶正電

絲綢帶負電

這個實驗展示了電子的轉移

Example Exercises

Example 1: Calculating Atomic Charge

Calculate the electric charge of an atom with 6 electrons and 3 protons:

Q = -6 × 1.6 × 10^-19 + 3 × 1.6 × 10^-19

Q = -4.8 × 10^-19 C

The atom has a net negative charge

Example 2: Charge Removal

Calculate the final charge after removing -1.5 microcoulombs:

Initial charge = -1.5 × 10^-6 C

Removed charge = -1.5 × 10^-6 C

Final charge = 0 C (neutral)

範例練習

範例1：計算原子電量

計算帶有6個電子和3個質子的原子電量：

Q = -6 × 1.6 × 10^-19 + 3 × 1.6 × 10^-19

Q = -4.8 × 10^-19 C

該原子帶有淨負電荷

範例2：電荷移除

計算移去-1.5微庫侖電荷後的帶電量：

初始電量 = -1.5 × 10^-6 C

移除電量 = -1.5 × 10^-6 C

最終電量 = 0 C（中性）

Summary

Static charge is electric charge at rest

Like charges repel, opposite charges attract

Electrostatic induction affects neutral objects

Charge can be calculated using the number of electrons and protons

Understanding static charge is fundamental to electricity

總結

靜電荷是靜止的電荷

同性相斥，異性相吸

靜電感應影響中性物體

電量可以通過電子和質子數量計算

理解靜電荷是電學的基礎

High School Basic Electricity: Current Direction and Magnitude - 高中基本電學：電流的方向與大小

Posted on 2024-03-25 Edited on 2025-05-05 In Electronics , Physics , Basic Electronics Views: Disqus:

Introduction

Understanding current direction and magnitude is fundamental to electrical engineering. This article explores the basic concepts of current flow, its direction, and how to calculate its magnitude in practical applications. We will cover both theoretical concepts and practical examples to help you better understand current in electrical circuits.

引言

理解電流的方向和大小是電機工程的基本要素。本文將探討電流流動的基本概念、方向以及如何在實際應用中計算其大小。我們將涵蓋理論概念和實際範例，幫助您更好地理解電路中的電流。

Definition of Current

Current is defined as the rate of flow of electric charge. It represents the amount of charge passing through a conductor's cross-section per unit time. This fundamental concept is essential for understanding how electricity works in circuits.

Current is measured in amperes (A)

One ampere equals one coulomb per second

Current can be either direct (DC) or alternating (AC)

電流的定義

電流被定義為電荷流動的速率。它代表單位時間內通過導體截面的電荷量。這個基本概念對於理解電路中電的工作原理至關重要。

Magnitude of Current

Basic Formula

The magnitude of current is calculated using the formula:

I = Q/t

Where:

I = Current in amperes (A)

Q = Charge in coulombs (C)

t = Time in seconds (s)

Important Relationships

Current is directly proportional to charge (I ∝ Q)

Current is inversely proportional to time (I ∝ 1/t)

Current can be measured using an ammeter

Current is a fundamental quantity in electrical circuits

High School Basic Electricity: Voltage - 高中基本電學：電壓

Posted on 2024-03-25 Edited on 2025-05-05 In Electronics Views: Disqus:

Introduction

Voltage is one of the fundamental concepts in electricity, playing a crucial role in understanding how electrical circuits work. This article will explain voltage through everyday examples and analogies, making it easier to grasp this important concept.

引言

電壓是電學中的基本概念之一，對於理解電路運作起著至關重要的作用。本文將通過日常生活中的例子和類比來解釋電壓，使這個重要概念更容易理解。

What is Voltage?

Voltage can be understood through a simple water analogy:

  • Just as water flows from high to low levels, electricity flows from high to low voltage
  • Water pressure pushes water through pipes, voltage pushes electrons through wires
  • The greater the height difference, the stronger the water flow; similarly, the greater the voltage difference, the stronger the current

什麼是電壓？

電壓可以通過簡單的水流類比來理解：

  • 就像水從高處流向低處，電流從高電壓流向低電壓
  • 水壓推動水通過管道，電壓推動電子通過導線
  • 高度差越大，水流越強；同樣，電壓差越大，電流越強

Definition of Voltage

Voltage is defined as the energy required to move electric charges in a circuit. It is measured in volts (V) and represents the potential energy difference between two points in an electrical circuit.

電壓的定義

電壓被定義為在電路中移動電荷所需的能量。它以伏特(V)為單位，代表電路中兩點之間的電位能差。

Types of Voltage

  • Electric Potential: The potential energy at a specific point in an electric field
  • Potential Difference: The difference in electric potential between two points
  • Electromotive Force (EMF): The energy provided by a power source to move charges
  • Voltage Drop: The decrease in voltage across a component in a circuit

電壓的類型

  • 電位：電場中特定點的電位能
  • 電位差：兩點之間的電位差異
  • 電動勢：電源提供的移動電荷的能量
  • 電壓降：電路中元件兩端的電壓降低

Key Concepts

Voltage and Current Flow

Voltage is the driving force behind current flow, similar to how water pressure drives water flow. The relationship can be expressed as:

VAB = VA - VB

For example, if point A has a potential of 100V and point B has 60V:

VAB = 100V - 60V = 40V

重要概念

電壓與電流流動

電壓是推動電流流動的力量，類似於水壓推動水流動。其關係可以表示為：

VAB = VA - VB

例如，如果A點電位為100V，B點電位為60V：

VAB = 100V - 60V = 40V

Unit of Voltage

The volt (V) is the SI unit of voltage, named after Alessandro Volta, who invented the first chemical battery. One volt is defined as the potential difference that will drive one ampere of current through one ohm of resistance.

電壓的單位

伏特(V)是電壓的國際單位，以發明第一個化學電池的亞歷山大·伏特命名。一伏特被定義為能夠驅動一安培電流通過一歐姆電阻的電位差。

Work and Energy

English:

  • Use italics for symbols and upright font for units.
