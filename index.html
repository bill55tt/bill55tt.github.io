High School Basic Electricity: Current Direction and Magnitude - 高中基本電學：電流的方向與大小

Posted on 2024-03-25 Edited on 2025-05-05 In Electronics , Physics , Basic Electronics Views: Disqus:

Introduction

Understanding current direction and magnitude is fundamental to electrical engineering. This article explores the basic concepts of current flow, its direction, and how to calculate its magnitude in practical applications. We will cover both theoretical concepts and practical examples to help you better understand current in electrical circuits.

引言

理解電流的方向和大小是電機工程的基本要素。本文將探討電流流動的基本概念、方向以及如何在實際應用中計算其大小。我們將涵蓋理論概念和實際範例，幫助您更好地理解電路中的電流。

Definition of Current

Current is defined as the rate of flow of electric charge. It represents the amount of charge passing through a conductor's cross-section per unit time. This fundamental concept is essential for understanding how electricity works in circuits.

Current is measured in amperes (A)

One ampere equals one coulomb per second

Current can be either direct (DC) or alternating (AC)

電流的定義

電流被定義為電荷流動的速率。它代表單位時間內通過導體截面的電荷量。這個基本概念對於理解電路中電的工作原理至關重要。

電流以安培(A)為單位

一安培等於每秒一庫侖

電流可以是直流(DC)或交流(AC)

Direction of Current

Conventional Current

Conventional current flows from positive to negative terminal

This concept originated from Benjamin Franklin's hypothesis in the 18th century

The conventional current direction is still used in circuit analysis and schematics

This direction is opposite to the actual electron flow

Electron Flow

In reality, electrons flow from negative to positive terminal

Electron flow represents the actual movement of charge carriers

Understanding both concepts is crucial for circuit analysis

Electron flow is important for understanding semiconductor behavior

電流的方向

傳統電流

傳統電流從正極流向負極

這個概念源自18世紀班傑明・富蘭克林的假設

傳統電流方向仍用於電路分析和電路圖

這個方向與實際的電子流動方向相反

電子流動

實際上，電子從負極流向正極

電子流動代表電荷載子的實際運動

理解這兩個概念對電路分析至關重要

電子流動對於理解半導體行為很重要

Magnitude of Current

Basic Formula

The magnitude of current is calculated using the formula:

I = Q/t

Where:

I = Current in amperes (A)

Q = Charge in coulombs (C)

t = Time in seconds (s)

Important Relationships

Current is directly proportional to charge (I ∝ Q)

Current is inversely proportional to time (I ∝ 1/t)

Current can be measured using an ammeter

Current is a fundamental quantity in electrical circuits

電流的大小

基本公式

電流的大小使用以下公式計算：

I = Q/t

其中：

I = 電流，單位為安培(A)

Q = 電荷量，單位為庫侖(C)

t = 時間，單位為秒(s)

重要關係

電流與電荷量成正比 (I ∝ Q)

電流與時間成反比 (I ∝ 1/t)

電流可以用電流表測量

電流是電路中的基本量

Practical Examples

Example 1: Calculating Average Current

If 3000 coulombs of charge flow through a conductor in 5 minutes:

Time = 5 minutes = 300 seconds

Charge = 3000 coulombs

Current = 3000/300 = 10 amperes

This represents a relatively high current flow

Example 2: Battery Charging Time

For a 1750mAh battery charged at 1 ampere:

Battery capacity = 1750mAh = 1.75Ah

Charging current = 1A

Charging time = 1.75 hours

This is a typical charging scenario for mobile devices

Example 3: Household Circuit

In a typical household circuit:

Standard voltage = 110V or 220V (depending on region)

Typical current = 15-20 amperes

Power = Voltage × Current

This current rating is for safety and practical usage

實際範例

範例1：計算平均電流

如果在5分鐘內有3000庫侖的電量流過導體：

時間 = 5分鐘 = 300秒

電荷量 = 3000庫侖

電流 = 3000/300 = 10安培

這代表一個相對較高的電流

範例2：電池充電時間

對於1750mAh的電池以1安培充電：

電池容量 = 1750mAh = 1.75Ah

充電電流 = 1A

充電時間 = 1.75小時

這是移動設備的典型充電情況

範例3：家用電路

在典型的家用電路中：

標準電壓 = 110V或220V（依地區而定）

典型電流 = 15-20安培

功率 = 電壓 × 電流

這個電流額定值是基於安全和實際使用考慮

Summary

Current is the flow of electric charge through a conductor

Conventional current flows from positive to negative

Electron flow is in the opposite direction

Current magnitude is calculated using I = Q/t

Understanding current is essential for circuit analysis

總結

電流是電荷在導體中的流動

傳統電流從正極流向負極

電子流動方向與之相反

電流大小使用I = Q/t計算

理解電流對電路分析至關重要

High School Basic Electricity: Voltage - 高中基本電學：電壓

Posted on 2024-03-25 Edited on 2025-05-05 In Electronics Views: Disqus:

Introduction

Voltage is one of the fundamental concepts in electricity, playing a crucial role in understanding how electrical circuits work. This article will explain voltage through everyday examples and analogies, making it easier to grasp this important concept.

引言

電壓是電學中的基本概念之一，對於理解電路運作起著至關重要的作用。本文將通過日常生活中的例子和類比來解釋電壓，使這個重要概念更容易理解。

What is Voltage?

Voltage can be understood through a simple water analogy:

  • Just as water flows from high to low levels, electricity flows from high to low voltage
  • Water pressure pushes water through pipes, voltage pushes electrons through wires
  • The greater the height difference, the stronger the water flow; similarly, the greater the voltage difference, the stronger the current

什麼是電壓？

電壓可以通過簡單的水流類比來理解：

  • 就像水從高處流向低處，電流從高電壓流向低電壓
  • 水壓推動水通過管道，電壓推動電子通過導線
  • 高度差越大，水流越強；同樣，電壓差越大，電流越強

Definition of Voltage

Voltage is defined as the energy required to move electric charges in a circuit. It is measured in volts (V) and represents the potential energy difference between two points in an electrical circuit.

電壓的定義

電壓被定義為在電路中移動電荷所需的能量。它以伏特(V)為單位，代表電路中兩點之間的電位能差。

Types of Voltage

  • Electric Potential: The potential energy at a specific point in an electric field
  • Potential Difference: The difference in electric potential between two points
  • Electromotive Force (EMF): The energy provided by a power source to move charges
  • Voltage Drop: The decrease in voltage across a component in a circuit

電壓的類型

  • 電位：電場中特定點的電位能
  • 電位差：兩點之間的電位差異
  • 電動勢：電源提供的移動電荷的能量
  • 電壓降：電路中元件兩端的電壓降低

Key Concepts

Voltage and Current Flow

Voltage is the driving force behind current flow, similar to how water pressure drives water flow. The relationship can be expressed as:

VAB = VA - VB

For example, if point A has a potential of 100V and point B has 60V:

VAB = 100V - 60V = 40V

重要概念

電壓與電流流動

電壓是推動電流流動的力量，類似於水壓推動水流動。其關係可以表示為：

VAB = VA - VB

例如，如果A點電位為100V，B點電位為60V：

VAB = 100V - 60V = 40V

Unit of Voltage

The volt (V) is the SI unit of voltage, named after Alessandro Volta, who invented the first chemical battery. One volt is defined as the potential difference that will drive one ampere of current through one ohm of resistance.

電壓的單位

伏特(V)是電壓的國際單位，以發明第一個化學電池的亞歷山大·伏特命名。一伏特被定義為能夠驅動一安培電流通過一歐姆電阻的電位差。

雙語內容範例 - Bilingual Content Examples

Atomic Structure

An atom consists of a nucleus containing protons and neutrons, surrounded by electrons in specific orbital layers. The number of protons determines the element.

  • Protons: Positive charge (+)
  • Neutrons: No charge (neutral)
  • Electrons: Negative charge (-)

原子結構

原子由含有質子和中子的原子核組成，周圍是特定軌道層中的電子。質子的數量決定了元素。

  • 質子：正電荷 (+)
  • 中子：無電荷（中性）
  • 電子：負電荷 (-)

Work and Energy

Work is defined as the energy transferred when a force moves an object through a distance. The formula for work is:

W = F × d × cos(θ)

Where:

  • W = Work (joules, J)
  • F = Force (newtons, N)
  • d = Distance (meters, m)
  • θ = Angle between the force and displacement vectors

功和能量

功被定義為當力使物體移動一段距離時所傳遞的能量。功的公式為：

W = F × d × cos(θ)

其中：

  • W = 功（焦耳，J）
  • F = 力（牛頓，N）
  • d = 距離（米，m）
  • θ = 力和位移向量之間的角度

Electrical Units and International System

Posted on 2024-03-24 Edited on 2025-04-06 In Science Views: Disqus:

Importance of Numbers and Units

English:

  • A number combined with a unit is the universal language in science, providing objective meaning.
  • For example, height and weight need units for expression.
  • Without units, numbers lose their context and significance.

繁體中文:

  • 數字加上單位是科學界的共通語言，能提供客觀的意義。
  • 例如：身高、體重等都需要單位來表達。
  • 沒有單位，數字就失去了上下文和意義。

International System of Units (SI)

English:

Base Units:

  • Length: Meter (m)
  • Mass: Kilogram (kg)
  • Time: Second (s)
  • Electric current: Ampere (A)
  • Thermodynamic temperature: Kelvin (K)
  • Amount of substance: Mole (mol)
  • Luminous intensity: Candela (cd)

Derived Units:

  • Speed: Meters per second (m/s)
  • Density: Kilograms per cubic meter (kg/m³)
  • Force: Newton (N) = kg·m/s²
  • Energy: Joule (J) = N·m = kg·m²/s²

繁體中文:

基本單位：

  • 長度：公尺 (m)
  • 質量：公斤 (kg)
  • 時間：秒 (s)
  • 電流：安培 (A)
  • 熱力學溫度：克耳文 (K)
  • 物質的量：莫耳 (mol)
  • 發光強度：燭光 (cd)

導出單位：

  • 速度：米每秒 (m/s)
  • 密度：公斤每立方公尺 (kg/m³)
  • 力：牛頓 (N) = kg·m/s²
  • 能量：焦耳 (J) = N·m = kg·m²/s²

Writing Conventions

English:

  • Use italics for symbols and upright font for units.
  • Example: “The current I = 5 A flows through a resistor of resistance R = 10 Ω.”
  • Leave a space between the number and the unit.
  • Units derived from proper names are capitalized (e.g., Ampere → A), but written in lowercase when spelled out (e.g., ampere).

繁體中文:

  • 符號使用斜體，單位使用正體。
  • 例如：「電流 I = 5 A 流過電阻為 R = 10 Ω 的電阻器。」
  • 數字和單位之間要留有空格。
  • 由人名衍生的單位符號要大寫（例如：安培 → A），但拼寫出來時用小寫（例如：ampere）。

Importance in Electrical Engineering

English:

  • Standardized units allow engineers worldwide to communicate effectively.
  • Prevents costly errors in design and implementation.
  • Enables accurate measurements and comparisons in experiments.
  • Forms the foundation for all electrical calculations and formulas.

繁體中文:

  • 標準化的單位使全球工程師能有效溝通。
  • 防止設計和實施過程中的昂貴錯誤。
  • 使實驗中的測量和比較更加準確。
  • 為所有電學計算和公式奠定基礎。

Atomic Theory Summary

Atomic Theory Summary

1. Structure of Atoms

English:

  • Nucleus: Contains protons and neutrons.
  • Protons carry a positive charge, while neutrons are neutral.
  • Electrons: Carry a negative charge and orbit around the nucleus.

中文:

  • 原子核: 包含質子和中子。
  • 質子帶有正電荷，中子不帶電。
  • 電子: 帶有負電荷，圍繞在原子核外的軌道上。

2. Atomic Number

English:

  • Definition: The atomic number is the number of protons in an atom, which equals the number of electrons.
  • Example: Copper has an atomic number of 29, indicating 29 protons and 29 electrons.

中文:

  • 定義: 原子序是指原子中質子的數量，等於電子的數量。
  • 例子: 銅的原子序為29，表示有29個質子和29個電子。

3. Atomic Orbitals

English:

  • Maximum electron capacity formula: (2N^2), where N is the orbital layer number.
  • First layer (K layer): Can hold up to 2 electrons.
  • Second layer (L layer): Can hold up to 8 electrons.
  • Third layer (M layer): Can hold up to 18 electrons.
  • Fourth layer: Can hold up to 32 electrons.

中文:

  • 最大電子數公式: (2N^2)，其中N為軌道層數。
  • 第一層 (K層): 最多可容納2個電子。
  • 第二層 (L層): 最多可容納8個電子。
  • 第三層 (M層): 最多可容納18個電子。
  • 第四層: 最多可容納32個電子。

4. Valence Electrons

English:

  • Definition: Valence electrons are the outermost electrons that determine the chemical properties and reactivity of an element.
  • Example: Silicon has an atomic number of 14 and 4 valence electrons, meaning it typically forms four covalent bonds in chemical reactions.

中文:

  • 定義: 價電子是最外層的電子，決定元素的化學性質和反應性。
  • 例子: 矽的原子序為14，價電子數為4，這意味著它在化學反應中通常會形成四個共價鍵。

5. Charge and Mass of Particles

English:

Particle Charge (C) Mass (kg)
Electron -1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ 9.107 × 10⁻³¹
Proton +1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ 1.6729 × 10⁻²⁷
Neutron 0 1.675 × 10⁻²⁷

中文:

粒子 電荷 (C) 質量 (kg)
電子 -1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ 9.107 × 10⁻³¹
質子 +1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ 1.6729 × 10⁻²⁷
中子 0 1.675 × 10⁻²⁷

Summary

English:

  • Atoms are electrically neutral, as the number of protons equals the number of electrons.
  • The atomic number is a fundamental characteristic of an element that influences its chemical properties and reactivity.
  • Understanding the structure and properties of atoms is crucial for studying electricity and other scientific fields.

中文:

  • 原子是電中性的，因為質子和電子的數量相等。
  • 原子序是元素的基本特徵，影響其化學性質和反應行為。
  • 理解原子的結構和性質對於學習電學及其他科學領域至關重要。

Summary of Energy and Work

Posted on 2024-03-23 Edited on 2025-04-06 In Physics Views: Disqus:

Summary of Energy and Work

1. Definitions of Energy and Work

English:

  • Energy: The capacity of matter to do work.
  • Work: The process of energy transformation, measured in joules (J).

中文:

  • 能量: 物质做功的能力。
  • 功: 能量的转化过程，单位为焦耳 (J)。

2. Work Calculation

English:

  • Work formula:
    [
    W = F \times S
    ]
    • W: Work (Joules)
    • F: Applied force (Newtons)
    • S: Displacement (meters)

中文:

  • 功的公式：
    [
    W = F \times S
    ]
    • W：功 (焦耳)
    • F：施加的力 (牛顿)
    • S：位移 (米)

3. Example Illustrations

English:

  • Pushing a Handcart:

    • Applying 20 Newtons of force to move 4 meters.
    • Work = 20 N × 4 m = 80 J.

  • Diving Athlete:

    • Weight of 60 kg, potential energy calculation:
      [
      F = m \times g = 60 , \text{kg} \times 9.8 , \text{m/s}^2 = 588 , \text{N}
      ]
    • Work done climbing 5 meters:
      [
      W = F \times h = 588 , \text{N} \times 5 , \text{m} = 2940 , \text{J}
      ]
    • During the dive, potential energy converts to kinetic energy.

Basic Electricity Chapter 1:PARTA Atomic Structure and Properties

Atomic Structure

  • The center of an atom contains a nucleus, which consists of positively charged protons and electrically neutral neutrons.
  • Negatively charged electrons orbit around the nucleus. For teaching purposes, different orbits are distinguished by different colors.
  • An atom is electrically neutral because the positive charge of protons in the nucleus equals and cancels out the negative charge of electrons outside the nucleus.
  • Electrons in the outermost orbit are called valence electrons, and their number determines the element’s valence in the periodic table.

Atomic Number

  • The atomic number represents the number of protons in the atomic nucleus.
  • In a neutral atom, the atomic number also equals the number of electrons outside the nucleus.
  • The periodic table is arranged according to atomic numbers.
  • Examples: Hydrogen (H) has atomic number 1, meaning it has 1 proton and 1 electron. An atom with atomic number 5 has 5 protons and 5 electrons.
  • Common element examples: Copper (Cu) has atomic number 29 (29 protons and 29 electrons), Germanium (Ge) has atomic number 32 (32 protons and 32 electrons), and Silicon (Si) has atomic number 14 (14 protons and 14 electrons).

Electron Orbital Distribution

  • Electrons outside the nucleus are distributed in different orbits in layers.
  • The maximum number of electrons each layer can hold can be calculated using the formula 2n², where n is the orbit number (n=1, 2, 3, 4…).
  • This formula is only applicable for orbits n ≤ 4.
  • The first layer (n=1), also called K shell, can hold up to 2 × (1)² = 2 electrons.
  • The second layer (n=2), also called L shell, can hold up to 2 × (2)² = 8 electrons.
  • The third layer (n=3), also called M shell, can hold up to 2 × (3)² = 18 electrons.
  • The fourth layer (n=4), also called N shell, can hold up to 2 × (4)² = 32 electrons.
  • The outermost electron layer is called the valence shell.
  • Example: Silicon (Si) has atomic number 14, with electron distribution: 2 electrons in K shell, 8 electrons in L shell, and 4 electrons in M shell. Therefore, silicon has 4 valence electrons and is a tetravalent atom.

Electric Charge and Mass

  • The unit of electric charge is Coulomb (C).
  • Electrons carry negative charge of -1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ Coulombs.
  • Protons carry positive charge of +1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ Coulombs. The charge magnitudes of electrons and protons are equal but opposite in sign.
  • Neutrons carry no charge (0).
  • The unit of mass is kilograms (kg).
  • Electron mass is approximately 9.107 × 10⁻³¹ kg.
  • Proton mass is approximately 1.6729 × 10⁻²⁷ kg.
  • Proton mass is much larger than electron mass, about 1840 times greater.

Summary and Recommendations

  • This lesson introduced the basic structure of atoms.
  • Students are recommended to practice drawing atomic structures of Copper (Cu), Germanium (Ge), and Silicon (Si), showing electron distribution and valence electrons, using the 2n² formula.

基本電學 第一章：PARTA 原子結構與特性

原子結構

  • 原子中心有一個原子核，原子核由帶正電荷的質子和不帶電荷的中子組成。
  • 帶負電荷的電子在原子核外的軌道上運行。為了方便講解，軌道會用不同顏色區分。
  • 原子是電中性的，因為原子核內質子的正電荷與核外電子的負電荷數量相等且互相抵消。
  • 最外層軌道的電子稱為價電子，價電子的數量決定了該元素在元素週期表上的價數。

原子序

  • 原子序代表了該原子核中的質子數量。
  • 在電中性的原子中，原子序也等於核外電子的數量。
  • 元素週期表是根據原子序排列的。
  • 例如：原子序為 1 的氫原子，其原子核中含有 1 個質子，核外也有 1 個電子。原子序為 5 的原子，其原子核中有 5 個質子，核外有 5 個電子。
  • 常見元素的原子序例子：銅 (Cu) 的原子序是 29，表示其有 29 個質子和 29 個電子；鍺 (Ge) 的原子序是 32，表示其有 32 個質子和 32 個電子；矽 (Si) 的原子序是 14，表示其有 14 個質子和 14 個電子。

原子軌道分佈

  • 核外電子是分層分佈在不同的軌道上。
  • 每個電子層能容納的最大電子數可以用公式 2n² 計算，其中 n 是軌道數目（n=1, 2, 3, 4…）。
  • 這個公式只適用於軌道數小於等於 4 的情況。
  • 第一層 (n=1) 又稱為 K 層，最多可容納 2 × (1)² = 2 個電子。
  • 第二層 (n=2) 又稱為 L 層，最多可容納 2 × (2)² = 8 個電子。
  • 第三層 (n=3) 又稱為 M 層，最多可容納 2 × (3)² = 18 個電子。
  • 第四層 (n=4) 又稱為 N 層，最多可容納 2 × (4)² = 32 個電子。
  • 最外層的電子層又稱為價層。
  • 矽 (Si) 的原子序是 14，其電子分佈為：第一層 (K) 2 個電子，第二層 (L) 8 個電子，第三層 (M) 4 個電子。因此，矽原子最外層有 4 個價電子，是四價原子。

帶電量與質量

  • 電荷的單位是庫侖 (Coulomb)。
  • 電子帶負電荷，電荷量為 -1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ 庫侖。
  • 質子帶正電荷，電荷量為 +1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ 庫侖。電子和質子的電荷量大小相等，符號相反。
  • 中子不帶電荷，電荷量為 0。
  • 質量的單位是公斤 (kg)。
  • 電子的質量約為 9.107 × 10⁻³¹ 公斤。
  • 質子的質量約為 1.6729 × 10⁻²⁷ 公斤。
